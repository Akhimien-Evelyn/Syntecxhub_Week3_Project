# HR Analysis Dashboard | Power BI
An interactive HR analytics dashboard built with Power BI, designed to help organizations monitor workforce trends, identify attrition risks, and make data-driven HR decisions.
# Project Overview
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of employee data across departments, age groups, education fields, and job roles. It was designed to surface key HR metrics at a glance while enabling dynamic filtering for deeper exploration.
## Key Metrics
| Metric | Value |
|-----------|-------------|
| Total Employees | 1,480 |
| Active Employees | 1,242 |
| Attrition Count | 238 |
| Attrition Rate | 16.08% |
| Average Age | 36.92 |
| Average Tenure | 7.01 years |
| Average Salary | $6.50K |

## Dashboard Features
### Attrition by Department
Bar chart comparing active employees vs. attrition count across **Research & Development**, **Sales**, and **Human Resources**.
### Employees by Education Field
Donut chart breaking down the workforce by education background:
- Life Sciences — 41.71%
- Medical — 32.77%
- Marketing — 10.06%
- Technical — 8.05%
- Other — 5.8%
### Job Role Breakdown Table
Matrix visual showing employee distribution by job role across satisfaction/performance levels (1–4), with totals.
### Attrition by Age Group
Combined bar and line chart tracking attrition count and rate across age brackets: **26–35**, **18–25**, **36–45**, **46–55**, and **55+**.
### Performance by Department
Donut chart displaying performance distribution across R&D, Sales, and Human Resources.
### Income and Trend by Job Role
Area chart showing monthly income and average tenure trends across all job roles.
## Interactive Filters
The dashboard includes dynamic slicers for real-time filtering:
- **Education Field**
- **Gender**
- **Business Travel**
- **Marital Status**
- **Salary Slab**

## Key Insights
- The **26–35 age group** records the highest attrition, signaling a retention challenge among mid-career employees.
- **Research & Development** has the largest headcount but also contributes significantly to overall attrition.
- Employees in **Life Sciences and Medical** fields make up nearly 75% of the workforce.
- **Laboratory Technicians** are the most represented job role with 199 employees across all performance levels.
- The **16.08% attrition rate** warrants targeted retention strategies especially for younger, early-tenure employees.
